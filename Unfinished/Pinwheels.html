On the Subject of Pinwheels

While these blow round and round, you just blow up.

Three pinwheels are shown, each with a button labeled "blow." underneath them. The pinwheels have different amounts of fins, of different colors. The colors of the stems will also vary. Each one will be slowly spinning. Use the features of the pinwheels to calculate whether or not to blow them. Pinwheels should be calculated and blown from left to right, unless there is a vowel in the serial number, in which case they must be calculated and blown from right to left.

Each pinwheel has a value associated with it, starting at 0, which must be calculated using all the rules below. If the pinwheel is spinning counter clockwise, change the sign of the value after applying all rules. If the value is greater than or equal to 0, blow the pinwheel the last digit of the seconds timer is equal to the least significant digit of the value. If the value is negative, do not blow the pinwheel. At least one pinwheel must always be blown- If there are no eligible pinwheels, blow the one with the most fins. If two or more are tied for the most fins, blow the left or rightmost pinwheel, depending on calculation order, within that tie. Blowing an invalid pinwheel, or blowing a valid pinwheel at the wrong time, will incur a strike.

The colors of a pinwheel's fins can be found in one of the sequences on the table below. Because the pinwheels are constantly spinning, it is impossible to tell where on the pinwheel the segment of a sequence starts. Once the correct sequence has been found, add the number shown to the pinwheel's value. The possible colors are Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple, and White. If the sequence shown on a pinwheel reaches the end of the sequence, it will loop to the beginning.
